    Gary Frank Career

    Gary’s career in acting began in 1974, when he was cast by director Richard Donner in Senior Year, a movie that went on and became the CBS television series, Sons and Daughters, alongside Glynnis O’Connor. Sons and Daughters revolve around young people changing society.

    He became a contract player at Universal Studios until the series was canceled after the first season. After two days, Gary was cast by director Mark Rydell and producer Michael Nichols to play Willie Lawrence in the critically acclaimed ABC series, Family of 1976, for which he won an Emmy Award for the Best Supporting Actor in 1977.

    Family had five successful seasons and also enjoyed a very successful run as a syndicated series in Europe during those years, as evidenced by the amount of fan mail he and the rest of the cast received from viewers overseas.

    Frank also starred in three episodes of Remington Steele as well as episodes of The Streets of San Francisco, T.J. Hooker, Charlie’s Angels, Fantasy Island, Magnum PI., Murder She Wrote, L.A. Law, Hill Street Blues, Friday the 13th: The Series, and guest-starred on Star Trek: Deep Space Nine as Yedrin Dax in the episode “Children of Time”.

    He played bombardier, Major Thomas Ferebee, in the TV film Enola Gay: The Men, the Mission, the Atomic Bomb, TV Christmas film The Gift: opposite Actor Glen Ford, and appeared on two episodes of Matlock (Season 1, Episode 3, “The Stripper”, September 30, 1986; Season 4, Episode 6, “The Clown”, October 24, 1989). In addition to his career, Gary has written a stack of scripts and is currently yet to publish his first novel.
