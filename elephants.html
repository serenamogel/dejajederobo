Overall Meaning

In ASL's song "Elephants," the lyrics speak about the challenges and struggles of a romantic relationship that has hit a rough patch. The singer expresses that they have been trying to ignore the warning signs that the relationship is deteriorating and that they both deny the issue of the metaphorical elephant in the room. The singer can see that the problems are weighing down on both of them every evening, but they are still trying to keep the issues on the fence and avoid facing them.


The chorus of the song, "It's just skin, bones, love" suggests that the relationship has lost its emotional depth and is now merely superficial. The singer can imagine losing the person they are in a relationship with completely, and that thought is the worst of it all. They then reveal that they have been drinking (had a couple, now I'm shooting doubles), and they will continue to do so because the other person has been cold towards them, kissing and looking over with the coldest shoulder ever sent.


The singer admits that they cannot win or lose the other person and that they are silent when the other person expresses what they like because they are out finding someone else. The singer wishes they could forget the painful memories that haunt them like elephants, but they can't.


Overall, the song "Elephants" portrays the challenges and difficulties of a relationship on the brink of falling apart. It highlights the struggles of holding onto someone you love when the relationship has become superficial, and both parties deny the issues in the relationship.
