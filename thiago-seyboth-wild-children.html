Thiago Seyboth Wild Children: Does Thiago Seyboth Wild Have Kids?

September 2024 · 3 minute read

Thiago Seyboth Wild children-Brazilian professional tennis player, Thiago Seyboth Wild was born on March 10, 2000 in Rio de Janeiro in Brazil.

At the 2018 Brasil Open, Seyboth Wild made his ATP main draw debut after being given a wildcard into the singles main event.

On November 3, 2019, in Guayaquil, he defeated Bolivian Hugo Dellien to win his first Challenger title. On March 1, 2020, he defeated Casper Ruud in Santiago, Chile, earning his first ATP Tour victory at the age of just 19.

He made history by being the youngest Brazilian to ever win an ATP title by taking this crown. He also became the first player to win an ATP Tour championship who was born in the 2000s.

Thiago Seyboth Wild career

On September 9, Wild joined Tiago Fernandes as the second Brazilian to win a junior Grand Slam (US Open). He also advanced to the junior semifinals at Roland Garros, the US Open, and the French Open doubles tournaments.

He won his first Challenger in Guayaquil in November 2019 when he was just 19 years old, breaking into the top 300 in the world for the first time.

With the victory, he shot up to position 235 in the world rankings and passed Thiago Monteiro and Joo Menezes to take third place in Brazil’s current tennis rankings.

He was given a wildcard to compete in the ATP 500 in Rio de Janeiro in February 2020. In the first round, he overcame Spaniard top 100 Alejandro Davidovich Fokina in three sets (5-7, 7-6 (7-3), and 7-5) in the longest match in the tournament’s history (3 hours and 49 minutes).

He lost to world number 32 Borna ori in the third set tiebreak in the following round. With this outcome, he rose to No. 182 and made his maiden appearance in the top 200 in the world on February 24.

The following week, when he received a wildcard invitation to the ATP 250 tournament in Santiago, Wild had his best season: he defeated Juan Ignacio Londero (world No. 63), Facundo Bagnis, and in the quarterfinals, Cristian Garn, the Chilean world No. 18 and champion of the Rio Open, who retired after dropping the first set in a tiebreak.

He beat Renzo Olivo in straight sets in the semifinals to advance to the final and become the youngest Brazilian to do so, surpassing the feats of former world No. 1 Gustavo Kuerten, who was 20 at the time, and Jaime Oncins and Thomaz Bellucci, who were both 21.

Since Bellucci finished in second place in Houston in April 2017, he also became the first Brazilian to play in an ATP-level final.

The Buenos Aires Challenger was won by Wild in singles and doubles on April 30. With that, he entered the world’s Top 200 in doubles for the first time and climbed back to the top 200 in singles.

He competed in the ATP Challenger Tour 175 event Piemonte Open in May and advanced to the quarterfinals before falling to top-seeded and top-50 player Sebastián Báez of Argentina in three sets.

Wild, who is ranked 172nd, made it to Roland Garros for the first time. He was among the players with the lowest ranking when the tournament qualifiers began.

He defeated Antoine Bellier, Riardas Berankis, and Dominik Koepfer in his three matches, dropping just one set in the process.

The biggest victory of his career came when he defeated second seed and world No. 2 Daniil Medvedev in a five-set battle to get to the second round.

Does Thiago Seyboth Wild have any children?

As of the time of filing this report, Wild has no children.
