Christopher Michael Pratt (born June 21, 1979[1]) is an American actor best known for starring in television and action films.

For his role as Andy Dwyer in Parks and Recreation (2009–2015), he received critical acclaim and was nominated for a Critics’ Choice Television Award in 2013 for Best Supporting Actor in a Comedy Series.

Career

At the age of 19, Pratt was waiting tables at the Bubba Gump Shrimp Company in Maui when he was discovered by actress and director Rae Dawn Chong. Pratt’s directorial debut was the short horror film Cursed Part 3, which was filmed in Los Angeles.

He played Harold Brighton “Bright” Abbott on the series Everwood. He also appeared in the 2008 action film Wanted, where James McAvoy beat him with a keyboard.

In 2009, Pratt became Andy Dwyer on the NBC comedy series Parks and Recreation. It was his major breakthrough role for which he got praised by many.

The actor, however, made his broad career, working in many hit films and series. He is the man who has been enthralling the larger audience with his marvelous performances.

Achievements

Chris Pratt is an award-winning actor. He has won innumerable awards for giving tremendous performances. Also, he has earned various nominations. Among many, he won the CinemaCon Awards for the category of Breakthrough Performer of the Year in 2013.
